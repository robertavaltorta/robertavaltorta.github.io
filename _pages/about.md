I am an assistant professor of Social Psychology at the [Pegaso University]() and an adjunct professor of Applied Social Psychology at the [University of Milano-Bicocca](). My main line of research focuses on **dehumanization**. My other research interests center around **gender-based violence** and **stereotypes**. Most recently, I have been working on people's understanding of and attitudes toward national and global **economic inequality**.

My academic journey began in 2008 with a BA in Communication and Psychology, followed by an MSc in Social Psychology, both pursued at the University of Milano-Bicocca. I completed my PhD in Social Psychology at the same institution in 2019, with a project on **dehumanization and the emotion of disgust**. In 2019, I was honored with the "Best Doctoral Dissertation in Social Psychology" award by the Italian Association of Psychology. 

Before starting my experience as an assistant professor, I worked for four years as a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Milano-Bicocca. Also, I spent a brief stint at the [Panteion University of Social and Political Sciences]() in Athens, where I studied dehumanization processes in political propaganda, and the [University of Valle d’Aosta](), where I was involved in a project investigating the role of digital technologies for people's well-being.
