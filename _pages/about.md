I am an assistant professor of Social Psychology at Pegaso University and an adjunct professor of Applied Social Psychology at the University of Milano-Bicocca. My main line of research focuses on **dehumanization**. Additionally, I am deeply interested in topics related to **gender-based violence** and **stereotypes**. Most recently, I have been working on people’s understanding of and attitudes toward national and global **economic inequality**.

My academic journey began in 2008 with a BA in Communication and Psychology, followed by an MSc in Social Psychology, both at the University of Milano-Bicocca. I completed my PhD in Social Psychology at the same institution in 2019, with a project on **dehumanization and the emotion of disgust**. In the same year, I was honored with the “Best Doctoral Dissertation in Social Psychology” award by the Italian Association of Psychology.

Before beginning my role as an assistant professor, I worked for four years as a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Milano-Bicocca. I also spent a brief period at the Panteion University of Social and Political Sciences in Athens, studying dehumanization processes in political propaganda, and at the University of Valle d’Aosta, where I contributed to a project investigating the role of digital technologies in people’s well-being.

From 2022 to 2024, I taught Group and Organizational Psychology at the University of Trento.
