I am an assistant professor of social psychology at [Pegaso University]() and an adjunct professor at the [University of Milano-Bicocca](). My main line of research focuses on <span style="color:red">**dehumanization**</span>. Additionally, I am deeply interested in topics related to **gender-based violence** and **stereotypes**. Most recently, I have been working on people's understanding of and attitudes toward national and global **economic inequality**.

My academic journey began in 2008 with a BA in Communication and Psychology, followed by an MSc in Social Psychology, both at the University of Milano-Bicocca. I completed my PhD in social psychology at the same institution in 2019, with a project on **dehumanization and the emotion of disgust**. In the same year, I was honored with the "Best Doctoral Dissertation in Social Psychology" award by the Italian Association of Psychology.

Before beginning my role as an assistant professor, I worked for four years as a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Milano-Bicocca. During this time, I also taught courses on group psychology at the [University of Trento](). Prior to my postdoctoral position, I spent a brief period at the [Panteion University of Social and Political Sciences]() in Athens, studying **dehumanization processes in political propaganda**, and at the [University of Valle d’Aosta](), where I contributed to a project investigating the role of **digital technologies** in people's well-being.
