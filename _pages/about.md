I am an Assistant Professor of Social Psychology at the [Pegaso University]() and an Adjunct Professor of Applied Social Psychology at the [University of Milano-Bicocca](). My main line of research focuses on dehumanization. My other research interests center around gender-based violence and stereotypes. Most recently, I have been working on people's understanding of and attitudes toward national and global economic inequality.

My academic journey began in 2008 with a BA in Communication and Psychology, followed by a MSc in Social Psychology, both pursued at the University of Milano-Bicocca. I completed my PhD in Social Psychology at the same institution in 2019, with a project on dehumanization and the emotion of disgust. In 2019, I was honored with the "Best Doctoral Dissertation in Social Psychology" award by the Italian Association of Psychology. 

Before starting my experience as a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Milano-Bicocca, I spent a brief stint at the Panteion University of Social and Political Sciences in Athens, where I studied dehumanization processes in political propaganda, and the University of Valle d’Aosta, where I was involved in a project investigating the role of digital technologies for people's well-being.
