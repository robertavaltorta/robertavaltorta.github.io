I am an assistant professor of social psychology at <a href= "https://www.unipegaso.it/" style="text-decoration: none">Pegaso University</a> (IT) and an adjunct professor at the <a href= "https://en.unimib.it/" style="text-decoration: none">University of Milano-Bicocca</a> (IT). My main line of research focuses on **dehumanization**. Additionally, I am deeply interested in topics related to **gender-based violence**, **stereotypes**, and **economic inequality**.

My academic journey began in 2008 with a BA in Communication and Psychology, followed by an MSc in Social Psychology, both at the University of Milano-Bicocca. I completed my PhD in Social Psychology at the same institution in 2019, with a project on **dehumanization and the emotion of disgust**. In the same year, I was honored with the "Best Doctoral Dissertation in Social Psychology" award by the Italian Association of Psychology.

Before beginning my current roles, I spent four years as a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Milano-Bicocca, where I also worked on **developing interventions to reduce household food waste**. During this time, I taught courses on social and group psychology at the <a href= "https://www.unitn.it/en" style="text-decoration: none">University of Trento</a> (IT). Prior to this, I conducted research on **dehumanization in political propaganda** at <a href= "https://www.panteion.gr/en/" style="text-decoration: none">Panteion University</a> (GR) and contributed to a project on **digital technologies and well-being** at the <a href= "https://www.univda.it/" style="text-decoration: none">University of Valle d'Aosta</a> (IT).
