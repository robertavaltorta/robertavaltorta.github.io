I am an assistant professor of social psychology at [Pegaso University](https://www.unipegaso.it/){:target="_blank"} (IT). My main line of research focuses on **dehumanization**. Additionally, I am deeply interested in topics related to **gender-based violence**, **stereotypes**, and **economic inequality**.

My academic journey began in 2008 with a BA in Communication and Psychology, followed by an MSc in Social Psychology, both at the [University of Milano-Bicocca](https://en.unimib.it/){:target="_blank"} (IT). I completed my PhD in Social Psychology at the same institution in 2019, with a project on **dehumanization in the intergroup and work domains**. In the same year, I was honored with the “Best Doctoral Dissertation in Social Psychology” award by the Italian Association of Psychology. In 2023, I received the “Young Talents” award from the [Accademia Nazionale dei Lincei](https://www.lincei.it/en/institution){:target="_blank"} for my research on **workplace dehumanization**.

Before beginning my current roles, I spent four years as a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Milano-Bicocca and taught courses on social and group psychology at the [University of Trento](https://www.unitn.it/en){:target="_blank"} (IT). Prior to this, I conducted research on **dehumanization in political propaganda** at [Panteion University](https://www.panteion.gr/en/){:target="_blank"} (GR) and contributed to a project on **digital technologies and well-being** at the [University of Valle d'Aosta](https://www.univda.it/){:target="_blank"} (IT).
